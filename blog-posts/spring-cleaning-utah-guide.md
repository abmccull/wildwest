## Why Spring Cleaning Hits Different in Utah

Utah's unique seasons make spring cleaning almost a necessity. Our long winters mean we've been cooped up inside, accumulating more stuff. Then comes the snow melt, and suddenly we can see our yards again – including all the things we meant to put away last fall.

It's okay to feel overwhelmed when you look around your home right now. Most Utah families are dealing with the same thing: winter gear that needs storing, camping equipment that needs organizing, and six months' worth of indoor projects that created more clutter.

## Your Month-by-Month Utah Spring Cleaning Plan

### March: Start Small and Build Momentum

**Week 1-2: The Warm-Up**
- Begin with one drawer or closet
- Sort items into three piles: keep, donate, trash
- Take donated items to Deseret Industries (locations in Salt Lake City, West Jordan, and Murray)

**Week 3-4: Tackle Winter Storage**
- Pack away winter clothes and gear
- Clean and store snow boots, coats, and accessories
- Check ski equipment for damage before storing

**Utah Tip:** March weather can be unpredictable. Keep one set of winter clothes accessible – you might need them again!

### April: The Big Push Begins

**Week 1: Basement and Storage Areas**
- Sort through storage boxes you haven't touched in two years
- Check for water damage from spring snowmelt
- Create an inventory list of what you're keeping

**Week 2: Bedrooms and Closets**
- Switch out winter and summer wardrobes
- Donate clothes that don't fit or haven't been worn in a year
- Organize accessories and shoes

**Week 3: Kitchen and Pantry**
- Check expiration dates and toss expired items
- Donate unopened, non-expired food to Utah Food Bank
- Reorganize cabinets and drawers

**Week 4: Living Spaces**
- Put away blankets and heavy throws
- Rotate seasonal decorations
- Clean out entertainment centers and bookshelves

**Local Resources for April:**
- Salt Lake City offers free bulky waste pickup for residents (call 801-535-6999 to schedule)
- Many community organizations organize spring cleaning service projects – great for neighborhood help

### May: The Home Stretch

**Week 1-2: Garages and Outdoor Areas**
- Sort through garden supplies and tools
- Check outdoor furniture for winter damage
- Organize sports equipment for summer activities

**Week 3-4: Final Touches**
- Deep clean areas you've decluttered
- Take final donation loads to local centers
- Schedule pickup for large items you can't transport

**May Special Programs:**
- South Jordan hosts their annual "Spring Clean-Up Day" (usually second Saturday in May)
- Many Utah cities offer free green waste disposal in May

## Where to Donate in Utah

Our communities have some amazing resources for donating items:

**Furniture and Household Items:**
- Deseret Industries (multiple locations)
- Habitat for Humanity ReStore (West Valley and Murray)
- Goodwill (locations throughout the valley)

**Clothing:**
- Local humanitarian services organizations
- Volunteers of America
- Local shelters and food pantries

**Electronics:**
- Best Buy recycling program (free for most items)
- Staples electronics recycling
- Utah County Health Department events

## Free Disposal Days by City

Here's when your city offers free disposal services:

**Salt Lake City:** Year-round bulky waste pickup for residents
**Murray:** Spring cleanup day typically in April
**West Jordan:** Community cleanup events in May and October
**Sandy:** Large item pickup available year-round with advance notice
**South Jordan:** Annual spring cleanup day in May

**Pro Tip:** Call your city hall in March to confirm exact dates and requirements. These programs fill up fast!

## Making It Manageable

Remember, you don't have to do everything at once. Here are some gentle reminders:

**It's okay to take breaks.** Decluttering can be emotionally exhausting.

**Progress isn't always visible.** Sometimes the biggest victories happen inside boxes and closets.

**Every item you remove makes a difference.** Even donating one bag of clothes helps both your home and your community.

**You don't have to keep things just because they were expensive or gifts.** If they don't serve your life now, it's okay to let them go.
