## The January Reality Check

It's okay to feel overwhelmed right now. Between the excitement of the holidays and the exhaustion that follows, it's normal to look at your home and wonder how it got so chaotic so quickly.

We see this every year in our Utah communities: families who had beautiful, organized homes in December are now buried under gift wrap, boxes, new toys, and decorations that need to find a home.

Here's the truth – you don't have to tackle everything today. But having a plan makes it feel much more manageable.

## Your Week-by-Week Post-Holiday Plan

### Week 1 (December 26 - January 1): The Quick Wins

**Focus on what's bothering you most:**
- Clear pathways through your home
- Put new gifts in temporary spots (not permanent homes yet)
- Gather all the wrapping paper and ribbons
- Box up Christmas cards to save or recycle

**Don't worry about perfection.** Just get your home functional again.

**Utah Tip:** Keep gift receipts in one envelope. January is prime return season, and you'll thank yourself for staying organized.

### Week 2 (January 2-8): The Big Sort

This is when you tackle the real work:

**Christmas Tree Disposal:**
Most Utah cities offer free Christmas tree pickup in January. Here are the key dates:

- **Salt Lake City:** Curbside pickup January 3-14 (no bags, remove all decorations)
- **Murray:** Special pickup January 9-20 (call 801-264-2600 to schedule)  
- **Sandy:** Drop-off at Sandy City shops building through January 31st
- **West Jordan:** Curbside pickup January 8-19
- **South Jordan:** Drop-off locations at city facilities January 1-31

**Pro tip:** Many Utah cities turn Christmas trees into mulch for parks. Your tree helps beautify our communities!

**Decoration Storage:**
- Sort decorations as you take them down: keep, donate, trash
- Replace broken ornament hooks and tangled lights
- Label boxes clearly ("Living Room Christmas" not just "Christmas")
- Take a photo of your tree before taking it down (helps with decorating next year)

### Week 3 (January 9-15): New Gift Organization

**The new stuff needs homes:**
- Toys: Set up a one-in, one-out rule with your kids
- Clothes: Go through closets and donate items that no longer fit
- Electronics: Properly recycle packaging and old devices you're replacing
- Books and games: Create space on existing shelves

**Utah families often receive:**
- Outdoor gear (skis, camping equipment) – organize in garage or basement
- Craft supplies – designate a specific storage area
- Kitchen gadgets – be honest about what you'll actually use

### Week 4 (January 16-22): The Final Details

**Deep clean areas you've organized:**
- Vacuum areas where the tree stood
- Wipe down surfaces that held decorations
- Put furniture back to regular arrangement
- Wash any linens that stored decorations

**Prepare for next year:**
- Make notes about what worked this year and what didn't
- Start a list of decorations you need to replace
- Plan any storage improvements for next Christmas

## Utah's Post-Holiday Disposal Resources

### Wrapping Paper and Cards
**Recyclable:** Plain wrapping paper, cards without foil or glitter
**Not recyclable:** Metallic paper, ribbon, bows, tissue paper

**Local recycling tip:** Most Utah grocery stores have expanded recycling bins in January specifically for holiday paper.

### Gift Boxes and Packaging
**Amazon boxes:** Break down and recycle curbside
**Electronics packaging:** Best Buy accepts Styrofoam and plastic packaging
**Large boxes:** Save good ones for moving or storage, recycle the rest

### Broken or Unwanted Decorations
**Still usable:** Donate to Deseret Industries, Goodwill, or local thrift stores
**Broken lights:** Hardware stores like Home Depot often recycle Christmas lights
**Artificial trees:** If beyond repair, most can go in regular trash (check with your waste company)

### Electronics Packaging and Old Devices
**Best Buy:** Accepts all electronics packaging and old devices
**Staples:** Recycles electronics and packaging materials
**Utah County Health Department:** Hosts electronics recycling events throughout the year

## Dealing with Holiday Overwhelm

If you're feeling stressed about all the new stuff in your home, remember this: **You're not obligated to keep everything you receive.**

**It's okay to:**
- Return gifts that don't fit your life or space
- Donate duplicate items
- Pass along toys your kids have outgrown to make room for new ones
- Recycle cards after enjoying them

**Teaching kids about post-holiday cleanup:**
- Make it a fun family activity, not a chore
- Let them choose which old toys to donate
- Explain how donations help other children
- Celebrate the space you've created together

## Common Post-Holiday Challenges in Utah Homes

### Problem: "We have too many new toys and nowhere to put them"
**Solution:** Use the one-in, one-out rule. For every new toy, an old one gets donated. Many Utah families bag up toys in good condition and take them to Toys for Tots year-round donation centers.

### Problem: "The artificial tree is too big for our storage space"
**Solution:** Consider a tree storage bag that compresses, or store sections in different areas of your home. Some Utah families store tree sections in the garage and decorations in bedroom closets.

### Problem: "We got duplicate gifts and gift cards we'll never use"
**Solution:** Many Utah retailers extend return policies through January. Gift cards can be sold on legitimate resale sites or donated to local charities.

### Problem: "Holiday decorations are taking over our basement"
**Solution:** This is the perfect time to audit your decoration collection. Keep what you truly love and use, donate items in good condition, and toss broken or worn-out pieces.

## Storage Solutions That Work in Utah Homes

**For Utah's dry climate:**
- Use airtight containers to prevent dust accumulation
- Cedar blocks help prevent moths in fabric decorations
- Avoid storing items in unheated garages (temperature swings can damage delicate ornaments)

**Maximize your space:**
- Under-bed storage works great for wrapping supplies
- Closet shelves can hold smaller decoration boxes
- Basement storage is ideal for larger items (but use shelving to keep boxes off floors)

**Label everything clearly:**
- Include the year you last used items
- Note which room decorations belong in
- Use clear containers when possible so you can see contents
