## The Call 2 Haul Program: Your Secret Weapon

This is Salt Lake City's best-kept secret for affordable junk removal. The Call 2 Haul program lets residents schedule pickup for large items at a fraction of private company costs.

**How it works:**
- Call (801) 974-6900 to schedule
- Available Monday-Friday, 7 AM to 3:30 PM
- Service area covers all of Salt Lake City proper
- Items are collected curbside (they won't come inside your home)

**What they'll take:**
- Appliances (refrigerators, washers, dryers, stoves)
- Furniture (couches, mattresses, tables, chairs)
- Electronics (TVs, computers, stereos)
- Large household items

**What it costs:**
- $25 for the first item
- $10 for each additional item (up to 5 total items)
- Maximum cost: $65 for five large items

Compare this to private companies that charge $150-$300 just to show up, plus $75-$100 per item. When I helped my neighbor dispose of an old sectional couch, broken dryer, and mattress set, Call 2 Haul charged $45 total. A private company quoted $380 for the same items.

**Pro tip:** Schedule during off-peak times (Tuesday-Thursday) for faster service. Spring and early summer are busiest, so book 2-3 weeks ahead during those times.

## Free Disposal Options That Save You Hundreds

### 1. Salt Lake City Transfer Station
**Address:** 1151 N 1500 W, Salt Lake City, UT 84116  
**Phone:** (801) 974-6900  
**Hours:** Monday-Saturday, 7 AM to 4 PM

This is where I take most of my household junk. For Salt Lake City residents, disposal is incredibly cheap:

- **Household waste:** $7.50 per cubic yard minimum
- **Appliances:** $25 each (they handle Freon removal)
- **Mattresses:** $15 each
- **Tires:** $5 each (limit 10 per visit)
- **Electronics:** Often free during special events

**Money-saving strategy:** Load up your truck or trailer and make one big trip instead of multiple smaller ones. I saved $150 last spring by consolidating three separate disposal trips into one.

### 2. Neighborhood Cleanup Days
Salt Lake City organizes free neighborhood cleanup events throughout the year. These are gold mines for disposal:

- Completely free for residents
- No limit on items (within reason)
- They provide dumpsters and sometimes accept hazardous materials
- Usually held in spring and fall

**How to find them:** Check the city website or call (801) 974-6900. They also post flyers in neighborhoods 2-3 weeks before events.

### 3. Electronics Recycling Events
Best Buy and other retailers accept electronics for free recycling, but the city also hosts special e-waste events:

- Free disposal of computers, TVs, phones, batteries
- Usually held at Liberty Park or other central locations
- Save $25-$50 per TV compared to transfer station fees

## Seasonal Timing Strategies That Save Money

### Spring (March-May): Plan Ahead
This is peak junk removal season in Salt Lake City. Everyone's doing spring cleaning after our long winters.

**Smart timing:**
- Schedule Call 2 Haul by early March for April pickup
- Private companies raise prices 20-30% during peak season
- Transfer station gets crowded on weekends – go weekday mornings

**What I did last spring:** Booked Call 2 Haul in February for March pickup. My neighbor waited until April and paid a private company $200 more for the same service.

### Summer (June-August): Best Private Company Deals
If you must use private services, summer offers the best rates:

- Companies offer 10-20% discounts during slower months
- Weather makes DIY disposal easier
- Transfer station is less crowded

### Fall (September-November): Perfect DIY Weather
Great weather for multiple transfer station trips:

- Comfortable temperatures for loading/unloading
- Less demand means shorter wait times
- Good time to clean out before winter storage needs

### Winter (December-February): Limited but Cheaper Options
- Call 2 Haul continues year-round
- Private companies sometimes offer winter discounts
- Transfer station is least crowded

## Money-Saving Hacks From a Salt Lake City Pro

### 1. The "Neighbor Network" Strategy
Team up with neighbors for shared Call 2 Haul trips. One call can handle items from multiple houses if they're placed at the same pickup location. Split the $25-$65 cost among everyone.

### 2. The "Craigslist Test"
Before disposing of anything, check if it has value on Craigslist or Facebook Marketplace. That "junk" might pay for your disposal costs. I sold a broken lawn mower for $40 – enough to cover my entire transfer station trip.

### 3. The "Donation Receipt" Method
Get receipts from Deseret Industries or Goodwill. Tax deductions can offset disposal costs for remaining items.

### 4. The "Bulk Pickup" Approach
Some private companies offer better rates for larger jobs. If you're doing a whole-house cleanout, get quotes for everything at once rather than piecemeal removal.
